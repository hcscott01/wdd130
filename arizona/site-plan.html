Home page

Welcome to the best website for AZ sports fans! Here You can see the wonderful colors of the Arizona teams, and a little history on these teams. These histories will be anything from amazing runs in their seasons, or times they won. When they were established as a team and much more. You will also get to learn a little more about the best athletes that our teams have to offer. This is an informative website to suck you into being a true Arizona sports fan.

Arizona Cardinals-The team was established in Chicago in 1898 as the Morgan Athletic Club, and joined the NFL as a charter member on September 17, 1920.The Cardinals are the oldest continuously run professional football franchise in the United States along with the Chicago Bears, are the only NFL charter member franchises still in operation.

Phoenix Suns-The Suns were one of two franchises to join the NBA at the start of the 1968 - 1969 season, alongside the Milwaukee Bucks. They were the first major professional sports franchise in the state of Arizona and would be the only one for twenty years until the Cardinals of the National Football League relocated from St. Louis in 1988.

Arizona Diamondbacks-The franchise was established on March 9, 1995, and began play in 1998 as an expansion team. The team plays its home games at Chase Field. Along with the Tampa Bay Rays, the Diamondbacks are one of the newest teams in MLB and are the youngest team to win the World Series.

Bios on the best of the best

Devin Booker - Devin Armani Booker was born on Ocotober 30, 1996, in Grand Rapids, Michigan. He is a shooting guard in the NBA. On June 25, 2015, Booker, was selected with the thirteenth overall pick by the Phoenix Suns in the 2015 NBA draft. He went on to make his NBA debut two days before his 19th birthday, in the Suns' season opener against the Dallas Mavericks. He became the first player in NBA history to debut at 18 years old after playing at least one year of college basketball. On February 12, Booker scored 23 points in a close victory for Team USA in the 2016 Rising Stars Challenge. The following day, he competed in the NBA All-Star Weekend's Three-Point Contest, becoming the youngest contestant to ever participate in the event and only the fourth rookie to compete in the 30-year-old contest. He finished fourth in the 2016 NBA Rookie of the Year Award voting and earned NBA All-Rookie First Team honors.

Kevin Durant- Kevin Durant was a high school basketball star and after playing college ball for only one season at the University of Texas, he was chosen second overall in the 2007 NBA draft by the Seattle SuperSonics. Durant went on to become a four-time scoring champion for the organization, which became the Oklahoma City Thunder after his Rookie of the Year campaign, and in 2014 he was voted the league's Most Valuable Player. Durant went on to lead the Golden State Warriors to back-to-back NBA championships, before being traded to the Phoenix Suns in January of 2023.

Kyler Murray- Kyler Murray was born on August 7th, 1997 in Bedford, Texas. He is a quarterback in the National Football League. Kyle Murray was selected in the first round with the first overall pick by the Arizona Cardinals in the 2019 NFL Draft. He will reunite with head coach Kliff Kingsbury, who had been recruiting Murray since his Sophomore year of high school. The pick also made Murray the first player to be drafted in the first round of both the NFL and MLB drafts. The selection will mark the first time that two quarterbacks from the same university have gone in the first round in successive seasons, with Baker Mayfield also being selected first in the 2018 NFL Draft. Murray is the first sub-six foot tall quarterback chosen number one in an NFL draft.

Corbin Carroll-Corbin Carroll was born August 21, 2000, in Seattle, Washington. He is an outfielder in the MLB. The Arizona Diamondbacks selected Carroll in the first round of the 2019 Major League Baseball draft. On September 20, 2023, Carroll became the first rookie in Major League history to record 25 home runs and 50 stolen bases in a single season. In Game 7 of the 2023 National League Championship Series, Carroll went 3-for-4 with two stolen bases, becoming the second-youngest player in MLB history with at least three hits and two steals in a playoff game, just behind Ty Cobb when he did it in the 1908 World Series at 21 years old. Carroll won the National League Rookie of the Year Award.
